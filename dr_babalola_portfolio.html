Profile Summary

My research focuses on developing reliability-aware detection, estimation, and classification frameworks using advanced techniques such as Hidden Markov Models (HMM/HsMM), entropy-based feature extraction, and time–frequency signal analysis, with demonstrated applications in low-SNR and complex signal environments. I have contributed to peer-reviewed work on wireless communication systems, including mobility-aware handover prediction, visible light communication (VLC) systems, and cross-layer reliability modelling, as well as signal detection and classification in acoustic and sensor network environments.

My research integrates estimation theory, machine learning, and communication system design to enable robust localisation, detection, and inference from real-world signals, supported by extensive MATLAB and Python-based simulation and algorithm development. I am particularly interested in advancing receiver design and opportunistic navigation using communication signals, including LEO satellite-based systems.

Teaching

Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy is grounded in the belief that engineering education should be experiential, inclusive, and intellectually engaging, enabling students to connect foundational concepts with real-world applications from the earliest stages of their academic journey. I emphasise conceptual understanding supported by structured problem-solving, ensuring students move beyond memorisation to develop deep analytical reasoning, with MATLAB and Python as primary computational vehicles.

Experiential Learning

Simulation-based labs, project-based assignments using real-world engineering datasets, and hands-on MATLAB/Python modules.

Interdisciplinary Integration

Connecting mathematics, physics, and computation — from linear algebra to signal processing to ML — within every course.

Inclusive Pedagogy

Multiple representations of material, scaffolded instruction, and strategies that support underprepared and first-generation learners.

Technology-Enhanced

Flipped classroom elements, Microsoft Teams, Blackboard, and a supplementary YouTube channel.

Full teaching philosophy available in the Teaching Statement (downloadable in the CV section).
